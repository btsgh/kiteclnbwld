## Shared Image World

The visual thesis for CleanBowled is "Nourishing Minimalism." Images must feel like high-end editorial lifestyle photography—airy, bright, and grounded in organic textures. The lighting logic is strictly soft, diffused natural light (golden hour or bright morning sun) that creates gentle, elongated shadows rather than harsh studio contrasts. 

Material language focuses on the raw beauty of vegetarian ingredients: dew-touched greens, matte ceramic bowls, linen textiles, and reclaimed wood surfaces. Backgrounds should be uncluttered, often utilizing the site’s warm cream (`#F9F7F2`) or pure white tones to create a seamless transition between UI and photography. Framing is deliberate and uncrowded; use top-down "flat lay" compositions for ingredients and eye-level, shallow depth-of-field shots for prepared meals. Human elements (athletes, mothers, wellness seekers) should appear in candid, mid-action moments—never looking at the camera—captured with a soft-focus background to maintain a sense of calm energy.

## Variation Policy

Variation is encouraged through subject matter to reflect the diverse customer base. While the "clean" aesthetic remains constant, the environment can shift: 
- **Athletes:** Focus on high-protein macro-ingredients (lentils, nuts, quinoa) in a bright, airy gym or outdoor track setting.
- **Postpartum/Recovery:** Focus on warming, soft-textured bowls (soups, porridges) in cozy, sun-drenched domestic interiors.
- **Pop-ups/Retail:** Use wider shots showing the interaction of people with clean, minimalist wooden stalls and organic packaging.

## Avoid

Avoid high-contrast "food porn" styling, saturated neon colors, or dark, moody "rustic" photography. Do not use generic stock photos of people smiling at salads. Avoid cluttered backgrounds, plastic containers, or artificial-looking studio lighting. Never use harsh shadows, busy patterns, or cramped compositions that violate the site's airy, premium editorial feel. No literal "medical" or "clinical" imagery for recovery sections; keep it focused on the holistic, natural food source.
